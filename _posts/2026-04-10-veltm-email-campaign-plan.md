This plan lays out exactly how we will build, launch, and measure a five-email nurture series paired with a conversion-focused landing page -- all inside Zoho One -- to drive qualified leads from the Seven Springs Country Club community in New Port Richey, Florida, into VELTM's paid trip planning service for multi-generational family vacations.

This revision targets a specific audience: affluent retirees aged 60-80 who live in Seven Springs Country Club, are planning summer vacations with their grandkids, and need help navigating the complexity of multi-generational travel. ## Campaign Objective

Convert Seven Springs Country Club snowbirds from initial interest into paid trip planning clients for summer 2026 family vacations. We are targeting grandparents who want to take their grandkids on meaningful travel experiences but find the logistics of multi-generational trips overwhelming -- different energy levels, safety considerations, age-appropriate activities, and the sheer complexity of coordinating a family group across international destinations.

The key message is simple: "You plan the family time. We plan everything else."

The conversion funnel starts with a low-commitment entry point and scales up based on trip complexity:

- **Trip Planning:** USD 25 per country planned -- the gateway offer. For a grandparent planning a single-country trip to Bali with three grandkids, that is twenty-five dollars to have a human concierge build the entire itinerary.
- **8-Hour Concierge:** USD 25 per country-day -- on-trip support during daytime hours. When you are navigating a temple complex in Thailand with a seven-year-old and a teenager, having someone a phone call away who speaks the local language and knows the area is worth every penny.
- **24-Hour Concierge:** USD 100 per country-day -- round-the-clock support for complex multi-destination family trips. For a two-week journey across Singapore, Bali, and Sri Lanka with grandchildren, this is the peace-of-mind tier.

These are the only prices we quote in any email. No approximations, no ranges, no "starting from" language. The SOP is explicit: service fees are non-refundable except when genuinely non-serviceable, and a 10% disbursement fee applies when VELTM pays on the client's behalf (unless waived). We do not add hidden margins. Retirees on fixed incomes hate surprises -- every email must reflect complete pricing transparency.

## Audience Profile: Seven Springs Country Club Snowbirds

Our target audience is highly specific: residents of Seven Springs Country Club in New Port Richey, Florida. These are retirees, typically 60-80 years old, affluent enough for country club living, deeply family-oriented, and likely traveling two to four times per year. Many are snowbirds who split time between Florida and northern states, so they are already comfortable with travel logistics at a basic level.

Their pain points around multi-generational travel are distinct. Planning trips with grandkids means coordinating across different age groups -- toddlers need nap schedules, teenagers want adventure, and grandparents want relaxation and safety. They want premium experiences but not stuffy or overly formal ones. They value safety and reliability above novelty. And many in this demographic are not tech-savvy enough to navigate multiple booking platforms, compare options across countries, or manage the logistics of international family travel on their own.

Their buying triggers cluster around summer: school is out in June, family reunion season peaks, milestone birthdays and anniversaries create "making memories" motivation, and the desire to give grandchildren experiences rather than material gifts drives spending decisions.

## Positioning Discipline

For this email nurture series, we lock the positioning to: VELTM is a travel concierge offering trip planning and on-ground concierge services in South and Southeast Asia, with a specialty in multi-generational family travel. The emphasis is on human concierges -- not AI, not chatbots, not automated systems. This audience wants to talk to a real person who understands their needs.

We position Southeast Asia as exotic but safe: Bali's family-friendly beach resorts, Thailand's cultural experiences with kid-friendly infrastructure, Sri Lanka's wildlife and beaches, Singapore's world-class family attractions, and the Philippines' island adventures. These are destinations that offer the "wow factor" grandparents want to give their grandkids while maintaining the safety and infrastructure standards this audience requires.

We do not claim 195+ countries. We do not claim AI-powered anything. We claim what we can deliver in the 8 confirmed markets: India, Indonesia, Philippines, Sri Lanka, Singapore, Cambodia, Malaysia, and Thailand.

## The Five-Email Nurture Sequence

1. **Welcome -- "This Summer, Give Your Grandkids the World" (Day 0):** Introduce VELTM as a travel concierge that specializes in family trips to South and Southeast Asia. Speak directly to the Seven Springs community -- "Your neighbors at Seven Springs are already discovering that the best gift you can give your grandkids isn't something they unwrap, it's somewhere they go." State the three service tiers and exact pricing. Lead with the USD 25 trip planning offer as the no-risk entry point. CTA: explore family-friendly destinations on the landing page.

2. **Value -- "Why Families Choose a Travel Concierge" (Day 3):** Address the specific complexity of multi-generational trips. Different bedtimes, different activity levels, dietary needs for little ones, accessibility for grandparents, finding resorts that have both a kids' club and a quiet pool. Explain how VELTM's human concierges handle all of this -- no hidden margins, transparent fee structure, local advisor expertise, and on-ground support. Emphasize: this is not a website where you book a flight and hope for the best. This is a person who builds your trip around your family. CTA: watch a short video of the family trip planning flow.

3. **Social Proof -- "Grandparents Who Gave Their Grandkids the World" (Day 7):** Testimonials from grandparents who traveled with grandkids through VELTM's service. Draft examples: "We took our four grandkids to Bali last summer. Ages 5 to 14. VELTM found us a villa with a private pool, booked a family cooking class, and when our youngest got sick, our concierge had a pediatrician at our hotel within the hour." And: "I was nervous about taking the grandkids to Thailand -- I'd never been to Asia. Our VELTM planner walked us through everything, from vaccinations to what to pack. The kids still talk about the elephant sanctuary." Feature partner hotel highlights across confirmed markets. CTA: see sample family itineraries from Bali, Thailand, and Sri Lanka.

4. **Destination Spotlight -- "Top 5 Family Adventures in Southeast Asia" (Day 10):** Showcase five family-friendly experiences across confirmed markets: (1) Bali -- family villa resorts with kids' clubs and cultural workshops, (2) Thailand -- elephant sanctuaries, cooking classes, and island hopping with calm waters, (3) Sri Lanka -- wildlife safaris, train rides through tea country, and beach resorts with shallow waters, (4) Singapore -- Gardens by the Bay, the Zoo, Universal Studios, and hawker center food tours, (5) Philippines -- island beaches, snorkeling in crystal-clear waters, and community homestays. All within the 8-country serviceability list. CTA: tell us which destination excites your family.

5. **Conversion -- "Your First Trip Plan is Just $25" (Day 14):** Direct offer -- start your family trip plan for USD 25 per country. Frame it as the easiest decision: "For the price of lunch at the club, you'll get a complete trip plan built around your grandkids' ages, your travel dates, and your family's interests." Include full pricing transparency: service fee is non-refundable except when genuinely non-serviceable, 10% disbursement fee when VELTM pays on client's behalf. Reinforce the human element: "Your planner is a real person, not a bot." CTA: confirm and pay.

- Hero image: grandparents with grandkids at a Southeast Asian beach resort. Warm, aspirational, not intimidating.
- Simple form: name, email, trip interest (dropdown of 8 confirmed countries), number of grandkids traveling, preferred travel dates (targeting summer 2026), and an optional "tell us about your family" text field.

## Zoho CRM Integration

Every lead entering the funnel gets a CRM record with custom fields tailored to this audience segment: Lead Source (email-nurture-sevsprings), Community (Seven Springs CC), Trip Type (Multi-Gen Family), Grandchildren Count (number field), Preferred Season (Summer 2026), Nurture Stage (1-5), Landing Page Visit (boolean), Concierge Tier Interest (trip-plan/8hr/24hr), and Deal Value Estimate.

Zoho Flow automation updates the nurture stage as Campaigns reports email engagement events. When a lead hits stage 5 and submits the booking form, a deal record is created in the Trip Planning pipeline with the stage set to "Qualification."

Lead scoring runs on a point system inside Zoho CRM: +10 for each email open, +25 for a click-through, +50 for a landing page form fill, +100 for a booking form submission. Leads scoring above 150 are flagged for immediate follow-up by an advisor.

## Never Event Guardrails

The Executive KPI Charter defines six Never Events -- outcomes that must never occur under any circumstances. These serve as hard campaign guardrails:

1. **Missed confirmation sent or promised to client.** If an email promises a response window, we must have the operational capacity to deliver. No aspirational SLAs. This audience will call if they do not hear back on time.
2. **Wrong pricing or terms committed to client.** Every price in every email must match the SOP exactly: USD 25 / USD 25 / USD 100. No creative rounding, no promotional pricing unless Carl explicitly approves.
3. **Guest arrives without valid booking.** The nurture sequence must never imply that filling out the inquiry form constitutes a confirmed booking. Grandparents booking for a group of six need absolute clarity.
4. **Untracked cash movement or charge outside approved balance.** Any payment-related language in Email 5 must reference the actual payment flow.
5. **Late refund with no logged reason.** Refund policy language must match SOP: non-refundable service fee except when genuinely non-serviceable.
6. **Major incident with no clear owner.** Every campaign asset has a named owner.
