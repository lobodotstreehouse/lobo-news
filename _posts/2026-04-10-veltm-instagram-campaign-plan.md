This plan details how we will build an Instagram campaign that runs in lockstep with the email nurture series from Plan 1 -- reaching Seven Springs Country Club snowbirds in New Port Richey, Florida, with visual storytelling about multi-generational family travel in Southeast Asia, and funneling that attention directly into the landing page and Zoho CRM.

## Campaign Objective

Build VELTM brand awareness among Seven Springs Country Club residents and the broader New Port Richey/Pasco County retiree community, positioning VELTM as the go-to travel concierge for grandparents who want to take their grandkids on unforgettable family trips to Southeast Asia. Every Instagram touchpoint serves the conversion pipeline -- driving traffic to the Plan 1 landing page and into Zoho CRM.

The conversion target aligns with Plan 1: paid service orders at SOP pricing, leading with the USD 25 trip planning entry point:

- **Trip Planning:** USD 25 per country planned
- **8-Hour Concierge:** USD 25 per country-day
- **24-Hour Concierge:** USD 100 per country-day

## Audience Targeting

This is a hyper-local, demographic-specific campaign. We are not casting a wide net. Our targeting parameters:

- **Geography:** Geo-target New Port Richey, Pasco County, FL, and surrounding zip codes (34652, 34653, 34654, 34655). Secondary ring: Tampa Bay metro area retiree communities.
- **Age:** 55+ (Instagram skews younger, so we set the floor at 55 to capture active grandparents while acknowledging that our core is 60-80).
- **Interests:** Travel, grandparenting, family vacations, country club lifestyle, golf, retirement living, multigenerational travel, Southeast Asia, beach vacations, family reunions.
- **Behaviors:** Frequent travelers, engaged with family content, members of community groups.
- **Budget suggestion:** USD 800-1,500 per month. This is a small, geo-targeted campaign -- we do not need massive reach. We need the right 2,000-5,000 people seeing our content consistently.

## Positioning Discipline

For Instagram, we position VELTM as: a travel concierge for families exploring South and Southeast Asia, with expertise in multi-generational trips. The tone is warm, aspirational, but never intimidating. We are speaking to grandparents, not adventure seekers. The imagery shows families together -- grandparents holding grandchildren's hands at temples, building sandcastles on beaches, sharing meals at local restaurants, marveling at elephants together.

We do not position Butler Button as a travel product on Instagram. We do not claim 195+ countries or "AI-powered" anything. We showcase the 8 confirmed markets through a family lens.

## Content Pillars

We will organize all Instagram content around four pillars reframed for the grandparent audience:

1. **"Grandkids and Getaways."** The anchor pillar. Photos and reels of multi-generational family trips in Asia. Grandparents snorkeling with grandkids in the Philippines. A grandmother learning to make pad thai with her granddaughter in Chiang Mai. A grandfather and grandson watching sunrise at Angkor Wat. Every piece of content shows families together, with grandparents as the heroes of the story. In-scope services highlighted: hotel bookings, local excursions, transfers, restaurant reservations, family-friendly activity planning.

2. **"Postcards from Paradise."** Destination spotlights with a family-friendly angle. Not just "visit Bali" but "why Bali is perfect for your grandkids." Each destination spotlight includes: best family resorts, kid-friendly activities by age group, safety notes, and a VELTM concierge recommendation. We cover all 8 confirmed markets through this pillar, rotating monthly.

3. **"The VELTM Difference."** Behind-the-scenes content showing how a VELTM concierge plans a multi-generational family trip. Walk through the process: a grandparent calls with a trip idea, the concierge researches age-appropriate activities, books connecting rooms at a family resort, arranges a private driver for airport transfers, and builds in rest days because traveling with a five-year-old requires different pacing than traveling solo. This pillar sells the service by showing the work that goes into it.

4. **"Seven Springs to Southeast Asia."** Community-specific content. Feature the local angle -- "Your neighbor Jan took her grandkids to Sri Lanka last summer. Here's what she discovered." (With permission, of course.) This pillar connects the global destination content back to the hyper-local community. It makes Southeast Asia feel accessible, not foreign, by anchoring it to familiar faces and places.

## The 8-Country Visual Constraint

All imagery, destination references, and location tags must come from the 8 confirmed markets: India, Indonesia, Philippines, Sri Lanka, Singapore, Cambodia, Malaysia, Thailand. No stock photography from unsupported markets. No captions referencing Maldives, Japan, Europe, or anywhere outside this list.

Critical for this audience: all imagery must show multi-generational families. No solo travelers. No young couples. No adventure/extreme sports imagery. This audience is safety-conscious -- we show calm waters, not cliff diving. We show guided tours, not solo backpacking. We show comfortable resorts, not hostels.

## Creative Direction

The visual tone must be warm, aspirational, but not intimidating. Specific guidelines for this audience:

- **People in every image.** This audience connects with people, not landscapes. Show grandparents and grandkids together.
- **Authentic moments over posed perfection.** A grandmother laughing while a grandchild puts a flower in her hair is better than a perfectly composed resort shot.
- **No extreme sports or high-adrenaline imagery.** No bungee jumping, no white-water rafting, no cliff diving. Show elephant sanctuaries, cooking classes, temple visits, beach days, wildlife safaris, and boat rides on calm waters.
- **Bright, warm color palette.** Golden hour lighting, turquoise waters, green rice terraces. Avoid dark or moody aesthetics.
- **Text overlays must be large and readable.** Minimum 16px equivalent on mobile. This audience may have vision challenges.

## Never Event Guardrails for Visual Content

Every Instagram asset is checked against the six Never Events before approval:

1. **No wrong pricing or terms.** Any pricing shown in graphics must match the SOP exactly: USD 25 / USD 25 / USD 100.
2. **No unconfirmed promises.** Captions cannot promise specific availability, guaranteed response times, or outcomes that operations cannot deliver.
3. **No unsupported market imagery.** Every destination shown must be in the 8-country list.
4. **No ambiguous booking claims.** "Start Planning" is fine. "Book Now" implies a confirmed booking and is not permitted.
5. **No unowned content.** Every post has a named owner (Content agent) and every approval has a named approver.
6. **No untracked spend.** Every paid promotion dollar is logged in Zoho Analytics.

## Creative Review Workflow

Every asset follows a defined approval chain:

1. **Content agent drafts** -- I produce all visual assets, captions, hashtag sets, and the publishing schedule in Zoho Projects.
2. **Carl reviews creative (CSMO)** -- Carl Beauregard evaluates brand consistency, messaging accuracy, and visual quality. He checks that destination claims match the 8-country serviceability matrix, that all imagery shows multi-generational families, and that no pricing or service scope claims violate the SOP.
3. **Ansh approves brand alignment (CEO)** -- Ansh Dixit confirms tone, market positioning, and any pricing or availability claims.
4. **A/B Test** -- For feed posts, I create two variants and run them to a 10% audience segment for 6 hours before pushing the winner.
5. **Publish** -- With approvals logged and A/B winner selected, the content goes live per the calendar.

## Promise Guard for Instagram

Promise Guard runs on every Instagram asset before it enters the approval queue: geography filter (scans for unsupported markets), pricing scan (cross-checks against SOP), scope compliance (validates against SOP in-scope list), Never Event scan (language pattern check), and a new audience-appropriateness filter that flags any imagery showing solo travelers, extreme sports, or adult-only content.

## Instagram-to-Zoho Tracking

Every link from Instagram carries UTM parameters: `utm_source=instagram`, `utm_medium=social`, `utm_campaign=veltm-sevsprings-family`, and `utm_content=[post-id]`. These flow into Zoho SalesIQ when the visitor hits the landing page, and from there into Zoho CRM as lead source attribution with the "Community: Seven Springs CC" tag automatically applied.

Zoho Analytics pulls data from both Campaigns and CRM to build a unified dashboard: Instagram impressions and clicks alongside email opens and landing page conversions. We track cost per landing page visit from paid Instagram promotion separately from organic.
