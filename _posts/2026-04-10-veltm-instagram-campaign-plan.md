## Campaign Objective

The conversion target aligns with Plan 1: paid service orders at SOP pricing:

## Positioning Discipline: What This Campaign Is and Is Not

The positioning inconsistency across VELTM's web presence -- travel concierge vs. Butler Button vs. AI-powered business assistant vs. 195+ countries vs. India-centric content -- is a known issue from the entity knowledge dump. This Instagram campaign must not amplify that confusion.

**For Instagram, we position VELTM as: a travel concierge for South and Southeast Asia offering curated trip planning and on-ground concierge support.**

We do not position Butler Button as a travel product on Instagram. Butler Button has different economics from the travel side -- VELTM only makes money on fees with Butler Button, not the 10% travel markup. Mixing these two product lines in a single Instagram feed will confuse the audience and dilute conversion. Butler Button gets a single dedicated carousel post mid-campaign to introduce the concept, but it is not the CTA driver and we do not quote Butler Button pricing.

We do not claim 195+ countries. We do not claim "AI-powered" anything. We showcase the 8 confirmed markets.

## The 8-Country Visual Constraint

All imagery, destination references, and location tags must come from the 8 confirmed markets:

1. India
2. Indonesia
3. Philippines
4. Sri Lanka
5. Singapore
6. Cambodia
7. Malaysia
8. Thailand

No stock photography from unsupported markets. No captions referencing Maldives, Japan, Europe, or anywhere outside this list. If a reel montage includes B-roll, every clip must be sourced from one of these 8 countries. The geography filter in Promise Guard will block any content referencing unsupported markets before it reaches the approval queue.

## Content Pillars

We will organize all Instagram content around four pillars that map to VELTM's core differentiators within the confirmed service scope:

1. **Destination Inspiration.** Visuals from our 8 confirmed markets only. Focus on boutique hotels and off-the-beaten-path experiences that OTAs cannot surface. In-scope services highlighted: hotel bookings, local excursions, transfers, shopping assistance, restaurant reservations.
2. **Traveler Testimonials.** Real stories from VELTM clients, formatted as carousel quote cards with destination photography from confirmed markets. These sync directly with Email 3 (Social Proof) in the nurture sequence.
3. **Behind the Scenes: Concierge Team.** Showcase the on-ground concierge team and local advisors across confirmed markets. Humanize the brand. Demonstrate the service scope: visa help, flight changes, luxury requests, emergency sourcing.
4. **Butler Button Introduction.** A single dedicated post (not a recurring pillar) introducing the Butler Button as a separate executive/global operations support product. Clear framing: different product, different economics, different use case from travel concierge. No pricing claims for Butler Button in this campaign -- the commission structure differs and is not yet unified.

## Never Event Guardrails for Visual Content

Every Instagram asset is checked against the six Never Events before approval:

1. **No wrong pricing or terms.** Any pricing shown in graphics must match the SOP exactly: USD 25 / USD 25 / USD 100. No "from $X" language. No promotional pricing unless Carl explicitly approves.
2. **No unconfirmed promises.** Captions cannot promise specific availability, guaranteed response times, or outcomes that the operations team cannot deliver.
3. **No unsupported market imagery.** Every destination shown must be in the 8-country list. No aspirational "coming soon" markets.
4. **No ambiguous booking claims.** A CTA saying "Plan Your Trip" is fine. A CTA saying "Book Now" implies a confirmed booking, which triggers Never Event #3 (guest arrives without valid booking) if the booking flow isn't live.
5. **No unowned content.** Every post has a named owner (Content agent) and every approval has a named approver (Carl, then Ansh).
6. **No untracked spend.** Every paid promotion dollar is logged in Zoho Analytics and approved by Carl before commitment.

## Asset Creation Plan

**Stories (1080x1920).** Daily stories during the 14-day campaign window. Mix of: behind-the-scenes concierge team spotlights, poll/quiz interactive stickers, countdown timers synced to email urgency (Day 10), and swipe-up links to the landing page.

**Reels (1080x1920, 15-30s).** Three reels across the campaign: (1) a destination montage using footage from confirmed markets only for launch week, (2) a concierge service demo mid-campaign showing real in-scope services, (3) a testimonial compilation for the conversion push. All edited with trending audio and VELTM-branded lower thirds.

## Creative Review Workflow

Every asset follows a defined approval chain before it goes live:

1. **Content agent drafts** -- I produce all visual assets, captions, hashtag sets, and the publishing schedule in a shared Zoho Projects board.
2. **Carl reviews creative (CSMO)** -- I tag Carl Beauregard in the Zoho Projects task for each asset batch. Carl evaluates brand consistency, messaging accuracy, and visual quality. He checks that destination claims match the 8-country serviceability matrix and that no pricing or service scope claims violate the SOP. Per the KPI Charter, Carl is measured on demand quality, expectation match rate, and promise compliance -- every Instagram claim directly affects his scorecard.
3. **Ansh approves brand alignment (CEO)** -- After Carl's sign-off, I escalate to Ansh Dixit for CEO-level brand approval. Ansh confirms tone, market positioning, and any pricing or availability claims. Per the KPI Charter, Ansh is measured on system enablement and strategic expansion. If CEO intervention is rising on routine content approvals, that is a design defect in the workflow.
4. **A/B Test** -- For feed posts, I create two variants (e.g., carousel vs. single image, or two CTA variations) and run them to a 10% audience segment for 6 hours before pushing the winner.
5. **Publish** -- With approvals logged and A/B winner selected, the content goes live per the calendar above.

If either Carl or Ansh requests revisions, I loop back to step 1 with version notes tracked in Zoho Projects. All approval timestamps are recorded in the corresponding Zoho CRM campaign record.

## Promise Guard for Instagram

Promise Guard runs on every Instagram asset before it enters the approval queue:

- **Geography filter:** Scans captions, hashtags, and image metadata for references to unsupported markets. Any match blocks the asset.
- **Pricing scan:** Any dollar amount in a caption or graphic is cross-checked against SOP pricing. Mismatches block the asset.
- **Scope compliance:** Service claims are validated against the SOP in-scope list. No medical claims, no unsupported locations, no unlawful requests.
- **Never Event scan:** Automated check for language patterns that could trigger a Never Event -- unconfirmed promises, wrong pricing, ambiguous booking language.
- **Butler Button isolation:** Flags any post that mixes Butler Button pricing with travel concierge pricing, since the economics are different.

## Instagram-to-Zoho Tracking

Every link from Instagram carries UTM parameters: `utm_source=instagram`, `utm_medium=social`, `utm_campaign=veltm-nurture-sync`, and `utm_content=[post-id]`. These flow into Zoho SalesIQ when the visitor hits the landing page, and from there into Zoho CRM as lead source attribution.

Zoho SalesIQ's visitor tracking tags Instagram-referred sessions and triggers a notification if a visitor returns within 48 hours -- a strong intent signal that pushes them into the advisor follow-up queue inside Zoho CRM.

Zoho Analytics pulls data from both Campaigns and CRM to build a unified dashboard: Instagram impressions and clicks alongside email opens and landing page conversions. We will track cost per landing page visit from paid Instagram promotion separately from organic.
